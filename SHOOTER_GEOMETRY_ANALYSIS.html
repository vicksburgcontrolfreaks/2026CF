1. Two Different Angles in Your Shooter

Figure 1: The ball's launch velocity is TANGENT to the wheel at the contact point, which is PERPENDICULAR to the contact radius. A 22° contact angle produces ~68° launch angle, while 28° contact produces ~62° launch angle.

2. The Physics Explanation

Why Launch Angle ≠ Contact Angle

When a ball leaves a spinning wheel:

  • The ball's velocity is tangent to the wheel surface at the contact point
  • The contact radius makes an angle with horizontal (what you're measuring)
  • The tangent is perpendicular to the radius
  • Therefore: Launch Angle ≈ 90° - Contact Angle
Geometric Relationship: Contact Angle (from horizontal) = θ_contact Launch Angle (from horizontal) = θ_launch θ_launch = 90° - θ_contact (approximately, assuming tangent release) Your case: 22° contact → 90° - 22° = 68° launch 28° contact → 90° - 28° = 62° launch Change: Launch angle DECREASED by 6°

3. Why You Need More Velocity Now

Figure 2: At angles above 45°, LOWER angles give LONGER range. Your 62° launch (28° contact) has worse range efficiency than 68° launch (22° contact).
Projectile Range Formula: Range = (v² × sin(2θ)) / g Where θ is launch angle from horizontal For angles > 45°: - Moving away from 45° reduces range - 68° is 23° away from optimal - 62° is 17° closer to optimal... BUT STILL SUB-OPTIMAL! Range efficiency: sin(2 × 68°) = sin(136°) ≈ 0.695 sin(2 × 62°) = sin(124°) ≈ 0.829 Wait... this suggests 62° should be BETTER!

⚠️ The Real Story: It's Not Just About Range

The range formula assumes the projectile lands at the same height it was launched from. But your speaker target is elevated!

For elevated targets, launch angles closer to 45° are NOT always better because:

  • Steeper trajectories (closer to 90°) have more time in the air at the peak
  • Allows the ball to "drop in" from above
  • Better scoring geometry (enters opening from top)

Your 68° launch was probably near-optimal for your specific target height and scoring requirements.

Moving to 62° sacrifices the high-arc "drop-in" trajectory for a flatter, faster shot that may be less reliable for scoring.

5. How to Measure Your Actual Launch Angle

✓ Practical Measurement Method

  1. High-speed video method:
    • Record shooter at 120+ fps with phone camera (slo-mo mode)
    • Capture moment ball leaves wheel
    • Freeze frame and measure velocity vector angle relative to horizontal
    • This is your TRUE launch angle
  2. Trajectory analysis:
    • Shoot at known distance (e.g., 3m from target)
    • Record shot with side-view camera
    • Measure peak height of trajectory
    • Use physics to back-calculate launch angle
  3. Protractor on robot (crude but fast):
    • Place protractor at wheel center
    • Draw line tangent to wheel at ball contact point
    • Measure angle of tangent line from horizontal
    • This is your launch angle
