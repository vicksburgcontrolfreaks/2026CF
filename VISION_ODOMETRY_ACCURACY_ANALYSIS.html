2.2 Camera Mounting Vibration (±1")

The sheet plastic mounting introduces dynamic positional error that varies with robot acceleration. This is the most challenging error source because:

  • Error magnitude changes with movement direction and speed
  • Cannot be calibrated out (non-deterministic)
  • Affects both cameras similarly during forward/backward motion
  • May cause Left and Right cameras to report correlated errors
Figure 2: Position error growth with distance. At 4m (typical competition range), ±1" camera vibration creates ~4" position error. Root-sum-square (RSS) combining shows total error.

Error Propagation Formula:

Angular error (radians) = camera_offset_error / distance
Position error = tan(angular_error) × distance ≈ camera_offset_error

At 2m distance: 1" vibration → ~2" position error
At 4m distance: 1" vibration → ~4" position error
At 6m distance: 1" vibration → ~6" position error

Impact: HIGH - This is your dominant error source at typical competition distances (2-5m). Vision standard deviation scaling partially mitigates this by reducing trust at longer ranges.

2.3 Plastic Warp Angular Offset (2-5°)

Angular offset from plastic warping creates a systematic rotational bias in camera measurements. This is particularly problematic because it's consistent rather than random.

Position error from angular offset:
Δx = distance × sin(angle_error)
Δy = distance × (1 - cos(angle_error))

At 4m distance with 2° offset: Δx ≈ 5.6" (dominant)
At 4m distance with 5° offset: Δx ≈ 13.9" (severe)

Critical Issue: Systematic Bias

Unlike random errors that average out, angular offset creates a consistent directional bias. Your robot's reported position will systematically drift in one direction during autonomous. This could cause:

  • Waypoint navigation missing targets by 6-14"
  • Auto-aim pointing off by 2-5 degrees
  • Difficulty scoring from long range

Impact: HIGH - This is your most serious error source. Unlike vibration, it cannot be filtered or averaged out. Requires physical correction or calibration.

2.4 Lens-to-Sensor CAD Offset (0.5-1.0")

You measured camera position to the lens rather than the actual image sensor. For the Arducam OV9281, the sensor is typically 0.5-1.0" behind the lens centerline.

Typical OV9281 dimensions:
Lens protrusion: ~0.3-0.5"
Lens to sensor: ~0.2-0.5"
Total offset: ~0.5-1.0"

This creates similar error propagation to vibration, but it's static and measurable. You can correct this by:

  1. Measuring actual lens-to-sensor distance (check camera datasheet)
  2. Updating PhotonVisionConstants.kRobotToLeftCamera and kRobotToRightCamera Transform3d values
  3. Adjusting the translation components by the measured offset

Impact: MEDIUM - Easily correctable by updating constants. Should be first fix.

2.5 Wheel Odometry Drift

Swerve drive odometry accumulates error over time from wheel slip, carpet friction, and module alignment. Typical drift is 1-2% of distance traveled.

3.2 Multi-Camera Pose Fusion

Located in PhotonVisionSubsystem.java:370-456

Figure 3: Multi-camera fusion averages independent measurements, reducing error by 1/√n. With two cameras seeing different tags, position uncertainty decreases by ~30%.
How it works:
  • Collects pose estimates from all cameras seeing AprilTags
  • Weights each pose by confidence (tag count, ambiguity, distance)
  • Performs weighted average of X, Y, and rotation
  • Reduces final standard deviation by 1/√n where n = number of cameras
Effectiveness against your errors:
  • ✓ Excellent against vibration (if cameras vibrate differently)
  • ⚠ Limited if both cameras on same plastic sheet (correlated vibration)

  • ✓ Excellent against random tag positioning errors

3.3 Ambiguity Filtering

Located in PhotonVisionSubsystem.java:484-490

How it works:
  • Rejects any single-tag pose estimate with ambiguity > 0.2
  • Ambiguity represents uncertainty in pose solution (0.0 = perfect, 1.0 = completely ambiguous)
  • Prevents clearly bad measurements from corrupting pose estimate
Effectiveness against your errors:
  • ✓ Prevents catastrophic failures from misaligned cameras
  • ⚠ May over-reject good measurements if angular offset is large
  • ✓ Protects against tag positioning errors causing ambiguous views

5. Recommendations for Improvement

Priority 1: Correct Lens-to-Sensor Offset (Easy Fix)

Effort: 15 minutes | Improvement: ±0.5-1.0" reduction

  1. Look up OV9281 datasheet or measure physical distance from lens center to PCB sensor location
  2. Update camera Transform3d constants in PhotonVisionConstants.java
  3. Adjust kRobotToLeftCamera and kRobotToRightCamera translation components
  4. Re-test in disabled mode and verify pose matches physical position

Why this helps: Removes a systematic offset that's easy to measure and correct. Gets you "free" accuracy improvement.

Priority 2: Field Calibration for Angular Offset (Moderate Fix)

Effort: 1-2 hours | Improvement: 2-5° heading correction, ±3-8" position improvement

  1. Place robot at known position in view of AprilTags (measure with tape measure to ±0.5")
  2. Record vision-reported pose and compare to actual measured pose
  3. Calculate angular offset: measured_angle - reported_angle
  4. Add rotation correction to camera Transform3d (adjust yaw component)
  5. Verify at multiple positions and distances
  6. Document final calibrated values in constants file

Why this helps: Removes the systematic angular bias causing directional drift. Most impactful fix for your setup.

Code location: PhotonVisionConstants.kRobotToLeftCamera and kRobotToRightCamera - adjust the rotation component of the Transform3d.

Priority 3: Reinforce Camera Mounting (Physical Fix)

Effort: 2-4 hours | Improvement: ±0.5-1.0" vibration reduction

Options (in order of effectiveness):

  1. Add metal backing plate: Sandwich sheet plastic between two aluminum plates with standoffs
  2. Increase mounting points: Use 4 screws per camera instead of 2
  3. Thicker plastic: Replace sheet plastic with 1/4" polycarbonate or aluminum plate
  4. Vibration damping: Add rubber/foam washers between camera and mount

Why this helps: Reduces dynamic error during high acceleration. Most noticeable during fast autonomous movements.

Priority 4: Remount Front Camera (Capability Restoration)

Effort: 1-3 hours | Improvement: Better coverage, ±30% error reduction from 3-camera fusion

Having 3 cameras active improves fusion and provides redundancy:

  • Error reduction from 1/√2 → 1/√3 (28% improvement)
  • Better coverage during rotation (reduces single-camera scenarios)
  • Fault tolerance if one camera loses track

Why this helps: More cameras = more averaging = lower error. Especially valuable during complex autonomous maneuvers.
